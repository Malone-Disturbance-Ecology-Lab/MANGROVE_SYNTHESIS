Mangrove Carbon Synthesis

Understanding carbon dynamics across mangrove-dominated ecosystems by combining eddy covariance CO2 and CH4 flux towers with remote sensing and reanalysis data into a general, transferable model.

Project goals

Mangroves are among the most carbon-dense ecosystems on Earth, but tower-based flux measurements are sparse and scattered across independent research groups. This project's goal is to understand carbon dynamics across mangrove-dominated ecosystems by assembling a multi-site synthesis of eddy covariance CO2 and CH4 flux records, paired with ERA5 meteorology, MODIS/Landsat vegetation indices, and canopy structure, to train a model that generalizes across climates, tidal regimes, and restoration states rather than describing any single site in isolation.

Contribute your tower's data

This synthesis is only as good as the sites in it. If you operate — or know of — an eddy covariance flux tower over mangrove, we'd like to include it.

Share your flux data, join the synthesis

We're looking for CO2 and/or CH4 eddy covariance records from mangrove-dominated sites of any duration, in any tidal or climate setting. Contributors are invited as collaborators on any resulting products, with full credit and co-authorship where appropriate — not just a data acknowledgment.
